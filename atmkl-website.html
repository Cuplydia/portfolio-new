The Challenge

Designing Khalilah Lyons' first website presented a unique challenge and opportunity to capture her business acumen, goals, and personality as a Diversity, Equity, and Inclusion (DEI) Professional, while also reflecting her expertise as a Product Designer and Front-end Developer. As a visionary entrepreneur, Khalilah's website needed to effectively communicate her brand identity to potential clients and stakeholders. Here's how the design process likely unfolded: Understanding Khalilah's Vision: The initial phase involved comprehensive discussions and interviews with Khalilah to gain insight into her vision, goals, target audience, and brand identity. This allowed the design team to align the website's design and functionality with Khalilah's aspirations and objectives in promoting diversity, equity, and inclusion.

Translating Business Goals into Design Elements:

With a clear understanding of Khalilah's business goals, the design team would have translated these objectives into tangible design elements.

This may have included creating a user-friendly interface, incorporating interactive features, and ensuring a seamless user experience across devices to effectively engage visitors.

Capturing Khalilah's Personality:

Given Khalilah's unique background and expertise, the website's design aimed to capture her individual style, creativity, and professionalism.

Custom graphics, personalized messaging, and visually appealing layouts likely played a role in reflecting Khalilah's personality and expertise as a DEI Professional, Product Designer, and Front-end Developer.

Balancing Design and Functionality:

As a Product Designer and Front-end Developer, Khalilah likely emphasized both aesthetic appeal and functionality in the website design.

Striking a balance between visually engaging design elements and practical features that enhance user experience and drive conversions would have been crucial.

Iterative Design Process:

Throughout the design process, the team engaged in an iterative approach, soliciting feedback from Khalilah and incorporating revisions to ensure the website met her expectations and requirements.

This collaborative process facilitated alignment between Khalilah's vision and the final website design, ensuring it effectively communicated her brand identity and expertise.

In summary, designing Khalilah Lyons' first website required a thoughtful and strategic approach to capture her business acumen, goals, and personality effectively. By understanding Khalilah's vision, translating business goals into design elements, capturing her personality, and balancing design and functionality, the design team was able to create a compelling and impactful website that reflects Khalilah's unique brand identity and expertise.

Planning & Discovery

In our planning and analysis phase, our goal was to figure out who would be coming to the website, what these users wanted / needed, and what features we should build. After we researching, planning, and meeting with our client, we created a list of features and enhancements, which included:

  • Clarifying her message (who she is and what she does)
  • Conveying a feeling of empowerment, excellence, and excitement
  • Highlighting her DEI presentations (videos, podcasts, public speaking)
  • Showcasing the list of Answer the Moment clientele
  • Telling the story / timeline of the Answer the Moment

Methods

We analyzed Khaliliah's existing marketing materials (PowerPoint for clients, e-List, LinkedIn, etc.)-
We analyzed similar DEI website like Jodi-Ann Burey (https://jodiannburey.com) - to determine what was working and what wasn't.-
Interviewed Khalilah (including developing an interview guide)

  • To get her input regarding what she was looking for in a website
  • To get a sense of her personality and a vision for how her website should be organized, what it should communicate
  • To get a Sense of her brand and aesthetic tastes.
  • Conducting online research on her LinkedIn to get a sense of her content, her values, her brand and her consulting firm.

Methods

  • Creating an interview guide
  • Organizing and conducting needs assessment and feedback meetings, asking follow-up / clarifying questions, and
  • Note-taking
  • Debriefing / compiling the findings from the interview with the team.

These included: We learned who was in charge (lol) Transferred her info to the new website. Looked at what we liked / didn't like. Divided up the tasks and created a design each. Evaluation

After we created our V1 prototype, we sent our link to the client for feedback.

Methods

Met / emailed with the client to presented our first version prototype Compiled feedback Created a list of next steps / follow-up tasks (collaboratively)

We created three different versions of the website throughout our design process.

Version 1

After the first round of feedback, our client liked the structure of the website and our design concept, but wanted more color and vibrancy. Design: more color; made sure to follow the style guide.

Version 2

Timeline: taking critical feedback and creating new versions Re-structured the news page to showcase different sections - press, featured and news Fundraising – thermometer Updated content / pics

Version 3

Managing the customers via nagging :). Putting fake info to prompt a response. Quote “customer may not know what they want, but they definitely know what they don’t like”. So we possibly can say we sketched different options for them to review, and adjust the directions every time we talk to them.

Deployment & Handoff

To deploy the site, we helped the client sign up with a hosting provider (GoDaddy) and exported / imported the WordPress site to the live server. We also ensured that the WordPress site was secure, that it was being backed up, and that all images were uploaded to a cloud hosting provider. Finally, we created training videos and a maintenance plan for the website handoff.
